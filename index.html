Wealth and Health Care

Over past few decades, there have been great advances in science and medicine that have lead to cures and treatments for many previously uncurable diseases. While this has gone a long way to improving the health of the country on a whole, increasing life expectancy and other metrics of health, it has created a new cause of health inequality: wealth. As you can see from the interactive charts to the right, spending on health care has increased at an incredible rate, and most of this increase is due to health insurance - specifically, private health insurance. This means that there now exists a large gap in life expectancy between wealthier people, who can afford good health insurance, and poorer ones who cannot.
